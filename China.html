Welcome to China

Top China Destinations

Great Wall of China

Stretching over 13,000 miles, this ancient fortification is one of the most impressive architectural feats in history. The Mutianyu section offers stunning views with fewer crowds.

Forbidden City

The imperial palace complex in Beijing that served Chinese emperors for nearly 500 years. With 980 buildings, it's the world's largest palace complex.

Terracotta Army

Thousands of life-sized clay soldiers buried with China's first emperor to protect him in the afterlife. Discovered in 1974 near Xi'an, this archaeological wonder dates back to 210 BCE.

Li River Cruise

The stunning karst landscape along the Li River between Guilin and Yangshuo is immortalized in Chinese paintings. A boat cruise offers breathtaking views of these limestone peaks.

Shanghai Skyline

China's most cosmopolitan city features the futuristic Pudong district with the iconic Oriental Pearl Tower and Shanghai Tower, the world's second tallest building.

Zhangjiajie National Park

The towering sandstone pillars of this UNESCO site inspired the floating mountains in Avatar. The glass-bottomed bridge offers thrilling views of the canyon below.

China Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) offer the most pleasant weather across China.

Spring brings comfortable temperatures and blooming flowers. Summer can be extremely hot and humid, especially in southern China. Autumn offers clear skies and comfortable temperatures. Winter is cold in the north but offers fewer crowds at major sites.

Currency & Payments

Cash is still king in China, though mobile payments (WeChat Pay/Alipay) dominate daily transactions.

Foreign credit cards work at major hotels and stores but not widely accepted elsewhere. ATMs are plentiful in cities. Exchange money at banks (passport required). Mobile payments require a Chinese bank account, so visitors should carry sufficient cash (Â¥100-300 per day).

Getting Around

China's high-speed rail network is the world's most extensive and efficient way to travel between cities.

Trains reach speeds of 350 km/h (217 mph). Book tickets in advance through Ctrip or at stations (passport required). Metro systems in major cities are cheap and efficient. Didi is China's Uber equivalent. Taxis are affordable but language can be a barrier outside major cities.

Cultural Etiquette

Understanding Chinese customs will greatly enhance your travel experience.

Greet with a slight nod or handshake. Present/receive items with both hands. Avoid pointing with fingers - use an open hand. Don't stick chopsticks upright in rice (resembles funeral incense). Try all foods offered as refusing may cause offense. Bargaining is expected in markets but not in stores.
