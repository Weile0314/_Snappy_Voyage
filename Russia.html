Welcome to the Russian Federation

Russia spans eleven time zones and two continents, offering travelers an incredible diversity of experiences from the cultural treasures of Moscow and St. Petersburg to the natural wonders of Siberia and the Caucasus Mountains.

Language

Russian is the official language. English is spoken in major cities but learning Cyrillic alphabet basics is helpful.

Religion

Predominantly Russian Orthodox, but Russia is a secular state with freedom of religion.

Currency

Russian Ruble (₽) ≈ $1 = 90₽ (as of 2023)

Climate

Varies from arctic in the north to continental in central regions to subtropical in the south.

Top Russian Destinations

Red Square (Moscow)

The heart of Russia and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, featuring the Kremlin, Lenin's Mausoleum, and the colorful St. Basil's Cathedral.

St. Petersburg

Russia's cultural capital with its canals, imperial palaces, and the world-famous Hermitage Museum. The White Nights in summer are magical.

Church of the Savior on Spilled Blood

St. Petersburg's most colorful church, built on the site where Emperor Alexander II was assassinated, featuring stunning mosaics.

Golden Ring

A loop of historic cities northeast of Moscow featuring onion-domed churches, monasteries, and traditional wooden architecture.

Solovetsky Monastery

A remote medieval monastery on the White Sea, once a fortress and later a Soviet prison camp, now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Trans-Siberian Railway

The epic 9,289 km train journey from Moscow to Vladivostok, passing through eight time zones and diverse landscapes.

Russia Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

Late May to early October offers pleasant weather, with June-July ideal for St. Petersburg's White Nights.

Winter (December-February) is cold but magical with snow-covered landmarks and winter festivals. Spring (April-May) can be muddy. September is great for golden autumn foliage. The Sochi area offers a subtropical climate year-round.

Currency & Payments

Russia uses the Ruble (₽). Carry cash as cards may not work due to sanctions.

ATMs are widely available in cities. Notify your bank before travel. US dollars and Euros can be exchanged easily. Credit cards from non-sanctioned banks work in major hotels and restaurants. Tipping 10-15% is customary in restaurants.

Getting Around

Russia's vast size makes air travel efficient for long distances.

The metro systems in Moscow and St. Petersburg are efficient, beautiful, and inexpensive. Russian Railways offer comfortable long-distance trains - book tickets in advance. Ride-hailing apps like Yandex.Taxi work well in cities. Driving requires an International Driving Permit.

Cultural Etiquette

Russians appreciate when visitors make an effort to understand local customs.

Dress neatly when visiting churches (women should cover their heads). Remove shoes when entering homes. Bring a small gift if invited to someone's home. Learn basic Russian phrases - even simple attempts are appreciated. Avoid political discussions unless you know the person well.
