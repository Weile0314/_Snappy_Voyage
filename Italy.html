Welcome to Bel Paese

Italy is a country of extraordinary beauty, rich history, and unparalleled cultural heritage. From the ancient ruins of Rome to the Renaissance art of Florence, the romantic canals of Venice to the stunning Amalfi Coast, Italy offers an endless array of experiences for every traveler.

Top Italy Destinations

Capri

The stunning island of Capri is famous for its rugged landscape, upscale hotels, and shopping, from designer fashion to limoncello. Don't miss the Blue Grotto, a sea cave illuminated by an otherworldly blue light.

Pompeii

The ancient Roman city frozen in time by the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 AD. Walk through remarkably preserved streets, homes, and public buildings that offer a unique glimpse into daily life 2,000 years ago.

Castel Sant'Angelo

This towering cylindrical building in Rome was initially commissioned by Emperor Hadrian as a mausoleum. Later used as a fortress and castle, it now offers panoramic views of Rome and houses a museum of military history.

Colosseum

The iconic symbol of Imperial Rome, this massive amphitheater could hold up to 80,000 spectators for gladiatorial contests and public spectacles. One of the New7Wonders of the World.

San Giorgio Maggiore

This 16th-century Benedictine church on the island of the same name in Venice was designed by Andrea Palladio. The church's bell tower offers spectacular views of Venice and its lagoon.

Ponte Della Libertà

The Liberty Bridge connects Venice to the mainland, stretching 3.85 km across the Venetian Lagoon. The bridge carries both road and rail traffic and offers beautiful views approaching Venice.

€2 - €5

Italy Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April-June) and fall (September-October) for pleasant weather and fewer crowds.

Spring offers mild temperatures and blooming landscapes. Summer (July-August) can be hot and crowded, especially in cities. Fall brings harvest season and beautiful foliage. Winter is ideal for skiing in the Alps and Dolomites, with fewer tourists in cities.

Currency & Payments

Euros are used throughout Italy. Credit cards are widely accepted but cash is preferred in small establishments.

ATMs (Bancomat) are widely available. Inform your bank before traveling. Many small shops and restaurants have minimum credit card purchase amounts (€10-€15). Tipping is not expected but appreciated for exceptional service (round up or leave 5-10%).

Getting Around

Italy has an excellent train network. High-speed trains connect major cities efficiently.

Trenitalia and Italo operate high-speed trains between major cities. Regional trains serve smaller towns. Driving is scenic but can be challenging in historic city centers (ZTL zones). Public transport is efficient in cities. Vaporettos are Venice's water buses.

Cultural Etiquette

Italians value politeness, style, and taking time to enjoy meals.

Greet with "buongiorno" (good day) or "buonasera" (good evening). Dress neatly when visiting churches (cover shoulders and knees). Meals are leisurely affairs - don't rush. Aperitivo (pre-dinner drinks) is a cherished ritual. Learn basic Italian phrases - locals appreciate the effort.
