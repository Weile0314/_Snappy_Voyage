Welcome to the Land of the Rising Sun

Japan is an island nation known for its blend of ancient traditions and cutting-edge modernity. From the neon-lit streets of Tokyo to the serene temples of Kyoto, the snow festivals of Hokkaido to the tropical beaches of Okinawa, Japan offers an incredibly diverse range of experiences for every traveler.

Language

Japanese, with some English in major cities and tourist areas

Religion

Shinto and Buddhism (often practiced simultaneously)

Currency

Japanese Yen (JPY) ≈ $1 = 110 JPY

Climate

Varies from subtropical to temperate with four distinct seasons

Top Japan Destinations

Tokyo

Japan's bustling capital where ultramodern meets traditional. From the digital art museum to historic Asakusa, Shibuya crossing to tranquil gardens, Tokyo offers endless discoveries.

Kyoto

Japan's ancient capital with over 1,600 Buddhist temples and 400 Shinto shrines. Famous for its geisha district, golden pavilion, bamboo forest, and traditional tea houses.

Osaka

Known as Japan's kitchen, Osaka is famous for its street food, nightlife, and friendly locals. Don't miss Osaka Castle, Universal Studios Japan, and Dotonbori's neon lights.

Hiroshima

A city of peace with moving memorials to its tragic past. The Peace Memorial Park and nearby Miyajima Island with its floating torii gate are must-see attractions.

Hokkaido

Japan's northernmost island famous for powder snow, hot springs, and national parks. Visit Sapporo for the snow festival and Niseko for world-class skiing.

Okinawa

A tropical paradise with white sand beaches, coral reefs, and a unique Ryukyu culture. The main island and surrounding islets offer amazing diving and a slower pace of life.

Japan Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March-May) for cherry blossoms or autumn (October-November) for fall foliage.

Spring brings mild weather and the famous cherry blossoms (late March to early April). Summer (June-August) can be hot and humid with rainy season in June. Autumn offers crisp weather and stunning fall colors. Winter (December-February) is great for skiing in Hokkaido.

Currency & Payments

Japan is still largely a cash-based society, especially outside major cities.

While credit cards are accepted at hotels and department stores, many restaurants, temples, and small shops only take cash. ATMs at 7-Eleven and post offices accept foreign cards. IC cards like Suica or Pasmo are convenient for transport and small purchases.

Getting Around

Japan's public transportation is efficient, punctual, and extensive.

The Japan Rail Pass is cost-effective for long-distance travel. In cities, subway and bus systems are easy to navigate with English signage. Taxis are expensive but clean and reliable. For rural areas, consider renting a car (international license required).

Cultural Etiquette

Japanese culture places great emphasis on manners and respect.

Remove shoes when entering homes and some traditional establishments. Don't eat while walking. Avoid talking loudly on phones in public transport. Bowing is the traditional greeting. Never stick chopsticks upright in rice. Learn basic phrases like "arigato gozaimasu" (thank you).
